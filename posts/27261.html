Bristol American Legion 19U squad falls to Simsbury on Sunday afternoon, absorbing first setback of the year in 10-1 defeat

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=27261 Published on 2024-11-20 09:47:41

By Michael Letendre

BRISTOL - The Bristol American Legion baseball team has used small-ball tactics over the past to pummel several quality opponents. 

But on Sunday afternoon, the program from Simsbury turned the tables, going station to station and base to base to cobble together a 10-1 victory from Muzzy Field in Bristol.

It was Simsbury’s first win (1-3) while Bristol fell to 1-1-1 overall.

Simsbury pitcher Evan Odegard (complete game, one run, three hits, five Ks) wasn’t overpowering but let his defense make plays behind him, earning the win in the process.

The squad scored at least one run over the second and sixth innings as crooked numbers ruled the day for Simsbury.

Bristol pitcher Caleb Molinsky (four innings, 69 pitches, four walks, two earned runs) battled, gave up just a half-dozen hits, fanned four while three errors did not help his cause. 

“I didn’t think Caleb pitched that badly,” said Bristol coach Jerry LaPenta. “We just played really poor defense. We gave them a lot of extra outs.”

Molinsky allowed two first inning walks but came back to produce a strikeout and pop-up to end the first half-inning.

Odegard countered, going 1-2-3 in the bottom of the first as the game was on.

And then in the second, Simsbury went on the attack.

With one away, Matt Fagnant singled to right and when the ball got away from the fielder, the runner took second base.

Nolan Conderino followed with a walk, stole second and the lead runner came home when an offering to third base was booted - allowing Fagnant to score to make it a 1-0 game.

And with runners on second and third and two outs, JJ Fagnant cleared the bases with a base hit to right, making it a 3-0 contest halfway through the second stanza.

Bristol got a runner during the bottom of the tilt as the debuting Nick Sconziano walked and then swiped second base to get into scoring position.

Jose Feliciano then beat out an infield hit to second - putting runners on the corners - and on a botched pickoff play, Sconziano scored from third to trim the deficit to 3-1.

But off one final strikeout, Simsbury got out of the jam to keep things a two-run game through two.

Simsbury opened the third with two consecutive base hits and off another miscue, the bases were juiced with no one out.

A slick 6-3 double play, cued by Elliot Norris at shortstop, wasn’t enough to get out of the frame.

Colby Wilhelm and Kyle Anthony came home to score off the double play and from an RBI single by Matt Fagnant as Simsbury roped up a 5-1 edge.

“The game should have been much closer than it was in the beginning,” said LaPenta. “We just didn’t play well defensively at all.”

Simsbury added to its tally in the fourth as a double steal saw Jackson Moore come home from third to give the visiting aggression a 6-1 push.

Ryan Maglio earned a one-out hit in the bottom of the fourth, but a 4-3 double play erased the runners as Simsbury ended the fifth frame ahead by five runs.

“We hit a few balls hard on the nose,” said LaPenta. “[But] they were right at guys. They got a couple bloopers.”

Post 2 turned to Gavin Colvin on the hill to open the fifth frame, but Simsbury remained aggressive.

With runners on second and third, a wild pitch scored Charles Orluk and Matt Fagnant dropped a slow roller by second base as the RBI tally scored Odegard to increase the Simsbury cushion to 8-1.

In the bottom of the fifth, Jose Feliciano absolutely crushed a ball towards the line in left, earning a double ,but pinch runner Mike Roalf never advanced as Simsbury took its seven-run lead into the sixth inning.

In the sixth, Odegard slammed a double by Jayden Churchill in center for an RBI and he scored when Matt Fagnant cracked a single to right - making the event a 10-1 affair.

Feliciano came in to pitch for Bristol in the top of the seventh, walking two, but not letting Simsbury add another run to the scoreboard.

And Odegard retired 10 of the final 12 batters he faced as Simsbury scored a nine-run victory on the road.

“Once we got behind, it kind of just sucked all the life out of us,” said LaPenta. “I didn’t expect to go undefeated, so we have to rebound tomorrow [against Northeast]."
