BC Wrestling earns seventh place finish at the Greater Hartford Open in West Hartford on Saturday

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=10660 Published on 2022-01-16 16:20:55

By Michael Letendre

WEST HARTFORD - The Bristol Central wrestling program battled some steep competition at the Greater Hartford Open from Hall High School in West Hartford on Saturday.

And the Rams made a quality seventh place finish, scoring 100 points and was able to top several other CCC programs that day.

East Hartford won the tournament behind 292 points while Haddam-Killingworth (168 points) was the runner-up.

Southington (164½ points) took third place, Killingly (146½) earned fourth while Ridgefield (137) nabbed fifth to pace a busy day of action.

Central had a couple grapplers battle into the final round which included stud Will Hamilton - earning the championship nod at 120 pounds.

And in the title bout, Hamilton shut out Ridgefield’s Alex Blaha by a 5-0 decision.

Hamilton, having an outstanding season, was joined in the finals by teammate Landon Dionne (138 pounds).

Dionne gave East Hartford’s Jaeckez Mendez all he could handle - and more - before the Bristol grappler fell via a 3-0 decision.

And in third place matches, the Rams had athletes in both the 132 and 220 pound consolations.

Central earned fourth place tallies as Aiden Lamarre (132) drew a no-contest against Victor Petkov from Ridgefield and at 220, Killingly’s Devin Exarhoulias pinned Central’s Michael Thaxter in 1:15.

This Wednesday, the Rams are back home - squaring off against Maloney in a CCC South confrontation at 6 p.m. - while on Saturday, Central travels to Xavier in Middletown for a little tournament action.

Meet time is 9 a.m.
