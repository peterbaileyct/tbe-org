On senior night, the Bristol Central wrestling squad defeats Edwin O. Smith before stumbling at Berlin the following night

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=11095 Published on 2022-02-05 10:39:43

By Michael Letendre

BRISTOL - The Bristol Central wrestling squad had a tough back-to-back challenge against Edwin O. Smith and Berlin this past Wednesday and Thursday, respectively.

The fun started against the Panthers as the Rams improved to 6-0 with a 37-28 win on senior night from the Charles C. Marsh Gymnasium in Bristol. 

Smith proved to be tough up and down the card but, with standouts like senior Will Hamilton at the ready, Central was able to square up another huge win.

At 113 pounds, Central’s Joseph Niek won via forfeit, as did teammate Julian Colon (120) before Hamilton (126) went to work.

The senior toppled Smith’s Caleb Phillips by a 12-4 decision while at 132, the Rams’ Aiden Lamarre topped Osei-tutu Hansen over a tough 11-9 decision.

Lincoln Archibald (138) earned a forfeit win while captain Umar Malick (152) earned a big come from behind decision.

Malick toppled the Panthers’ Dakota Blanchard over a 5-2 final over a tremendous finish. 

At 170 pounds, Jack Jones won via a forfeit decision and at 182, Central’s Jayden Colon defeated Alexander Barr with a 6-5 decision.

BC wrestling on Twitter said that Malick earned the ‘Animal Award’ for his come-from-behind victory . 

And then one night later on the road, the Central program lost for the first time this year, falling at Berlin, 62-15.

The Redcoats raised its ledger to 11-1 overall and 5-0 in the CCC South while the Rams dropped to 6-1 (4-1 CCC South).

Hamilton (126) pinned Jonah Lorenzo in only 12 seconds, Malick (160) drew a pinfall win over Mantas Malinauskas, and Jayden Colon (182) took a 4-3 decision over Berlins’s Tim Mrachek to lead Central.

The annual BC/BE wrestling meet - slated for the Thomas M. Monahan Gymnasium on Wednesday, February 9 at 6 p.m. - should be as competitive as it comes this year.

The Rams had a busy weekend if the schedule held because on Saturday, Central battled New Fairfield, Bethel, Haddam-Killingworth, Jonathan Law, Londonberry (NH), Masuk, Nonnewaug, Simsbury, and Harding from New Fairfield high school.

On the mats…Senior captain Will Hamilton is ranked #3 in New England and is simply a beast at 120 pounds. The grappler is also successful up the card at 126. The rankings were compiled as of January 26.
