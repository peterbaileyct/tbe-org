Okee visits the Bristol Adult Resource Center

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=16600 Published on 2022-09-02 07:00:00

By Laura Bailey

Freshly baked treats, beautiful plant life and an abundance of smiles greeted Okee the Fire Dog during her visit to the Bristol Adult Resource Center (BARC).

“Okee’s ready to make some friends today,” said Chris Hayden. “Come say, 'Hi'; she loves pets.”

Hayden, who played a key role in bringing Okee to the Bristol Fire Department, serves as her main handler. On weekends, the hard-working hound stays with Hayden and his family for some rest and relaxation.

“She likes coming home with me,” said Hayden. “On Saturdays, she gets a nice bath and manicure.”

Rescued and trained to be an emotional support dog by K9s for Warriors, Okee is the first fire peer support dog in the state.

More than a dozen community members at the Bristol Adult Resource Center took turns embracing Okee. The sweet-natured dog was presented with a basket of treats made onsite, as well as a fire hose dog toy.

“We made those treats right here in our dinning room,” said Mary Etter.

Etter has served as Executive Director of the Bristol Adult Resource Center for the past five years.

“We get about 70-80 people coming to this building a day,” said Etter. “I’ve been here 10 years and it’s honestly the best; I can’t imagine working anywhere else.”

Bristol Adult Resource Center is a non-profit group dedicated to providing services to, and improving the quality of life of, people with intellectual and developmental disabilities in the greater Bristol area.

Okee taste-tested one of the BARC treats made especially for her before giving enthusiastic approval. The 3-year-old Shepard-Hound mix became an official member of the Bristol Fire Department last week at her pinning ceremony.

“She just completed month one,” said Hayden, “and she’s adjusting pretty well. She just loves seeing people.”
