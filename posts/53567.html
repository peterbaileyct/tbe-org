This year’s Bristol Sports Hall of Fame inductees took varied paths to their honors- Part Three: Taylor Losey and Bob Fallon

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=53567 Published on 2024-11-11 07:00:00

The Bristol Sports Hall of Fame will be honoring eight inductees at the Hall’s induction dinner on November 22. This year’s honorees represent the best of Bristol in their varied accomplishments.

The inductees this year are Jeff Bashaw, Russ Carter, Bob Fallon, Taylor Losey, Bunty Ray, Beth Rotko, Rick Schmaltz and Cliff Snow III.

TBE is covering two honorees in each of a series of articles. This is the third of four in the series.

The two inductees introduced today are Taylor Losey and Bob Fallon

Taylor Losey

A state champion in two sports, Taylor Losey starred for Bristol Eastern in volleyball, basketball and track from 2002 to 2006.

She was an all-conference and all-state player in volleyball and a member of the Lancers 2005 state championship team.

Losey scored over 1,400 points in a stellar basketball career that included four all conference selections as well as All State honors.

She was the CIAC state champion in the javelin in 2006 and became the second Bristol Eastern female athlete to earn all-state honors in three different sports.

Losey was a four-time medalist in the Hershey North American Track and Field Softball throw event.  

After her high school career, she went on to score over 1,200 points at Pace University, attaining first team all-conference and all-Metropolitan honors. Losey led the Northeast Conference in free throw percentage in 2008 and 2010. She was the Darrell Halloran Memorial Award winner at Pace in 2010.

After her playing career, Losey has been a grad assistant basketball coach at Pace as well as a coach with the CT Magic AAU program.

Bob Fallon

A Massachusetts native, Bob Fallon came to Bristol to teach math at the Clara T. O’Connell School and began his coaching career as coach of the 7th and 8th grade basketball team in 1964.

He went on to coach freshman basketball at Bristol Central before embarking on a 23-year stint as golf coach at Central.

Fallon compiled a record of 136-85-4 as golf coach and his teams won 28 straight matches in the Colonial Conference from 1981-83. His 1983 team was 19-0 and the first undefeated team in BCHS history.

He was named State Golf Coach of the Year in 1989, when he coached Glen Carlson to the state tournament championship and an appearance in the Nationals in Arizona.

Fallon became involved in Special Olympics as a track and skiing coach in the late 1980’s and served as a Certified Master Level Track Official from 1993 to 2001. He worked at the Special Olympic World Games in 1995 and the National Sports Festival for the Disabled in 1999 and 2000.

Fallon served as coach of the Bristol Central Math Team for many years, but is most proud of his time with the Special Olympics and as a Board Member and treasurer of BARC.
