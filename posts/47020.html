MOVIA, INC. DONATES 4 ROBOT-ASSISTED INSTRUCTION SYSTEMS TO THE BRISTOL PUBLIC SCHOOLSBRISTOL, CONNECTICUT – June 10, 2024 – Movia, Inc. is pleased to donate 4 Robot Assisted Instruction (RAI) systems to the Bristol Public Schools in remembrance of members of the community who were lost in 2022.  An event in recognition of the donation will take place at the Ellen P. Hubbell School on Monday, June 10, 2024, at 10:00 am.

"We hope that these RAI systems can help children in the Bristol community and be another positive legacy of these beloved individuals.  This is a small gesture, but it is one that we can uniquely provide," stated Timothy Gifford, founder of Movia.

As a small business here in Bristol, Movia has shared in the losses to the community.  First among them is the tragic loss of young Nicholas Parenti who succumbed to a fast-moving brain tumor in August 2022.  This was a personal loss at Movia, as Rob Parenti, who worked with us for several years, brought his whole family on board to help us in our mission to support special ability learners through Robot-Assisted Instruction.  Margo Parenti and their two boys, Alex and Nick, were often in the office helping out.  Nick's light and happy energy are still felt in our headquarters on Federal Hill.  We miss Nick so much.

Then there was the unfathomable ambush of our finest first responders that led to the death of the two exceptional officers, Lt. Dustin DeMonte and Sgt. Alex Hamzy.  Although we did not have the privilege to know Lt. Dustin DeMonte and Sgt. Alex Hamzy personally, we honor their presence in the community and feel the loss to the Bristol Police Department and those who knew and loved them.

We would like to honor these members of the community by donating the use of our Robot Assisted Instruction (RAI) systems to the community in their name.

"We are grateful for Movia's generous donation and their mission to support students who require special services here in Bristol in a new, fun, and engaging way," stated Amy Martino, Director of Special Services of the Bristol Public Schools.

About MOVIA, INC.

Movia, Inc develops and deploys solutions using advanced robotics to reach the underserved community of both children and adults with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).  Movia began its mission in 2010 based in part on research at the University of Connecticut funded by the National Institute of Health (NIH).  Movia combines cutting-edge robotics with personal interactivity in a fully evidence-based solution to teach children and adults with ASD.  Movia moved to Bristol, CT in 2017.  For the past 7 years, Movia has worked closely with the City of Bristol on piloting systems in the school systems.  Bristol has been very supportive, providing Movia with economic development resources.  Please visit www.moviarobotics.com for more information.
