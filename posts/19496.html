Porrini is off to the New England Cross Country Championship after stellar showing at the Open

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=19496 Published on 2022-11-06 16:17:04

By Michael Letendre

MANCHESTER - Maybe Bristol Central cross country standout Frankie Porrini didn’t have the finish he envisioned at the 2022 CIAC Class L Championships last week.

But the senior harrier had a tremendous showing at the CIAC Open event from Wickham Park on Friday afternoon - making a top-15 finish and punching his ticket to the New England Championship.

The All-American Steeplechaser continues to impress on the cross country course this season as he moves on to yet another championship event.

He ended the day at Wickham with a stellar time of 16:30 over a loaded field.

“Bristol Central cross country’s Francis Porrini III is New England Championship bound after a 13th place finish at the State Open Championship,” said Central coach Tamara Stafford-Kirk on Twitter. “What a season!”

Porrini’s time was the fifth fastest of all the runners from the Central Connecticut Conference as Hall’s Isaac Mahler (15:53) was the top CCC finisher.

“Frankie was smart and ran a more conservative first and second mile to be able to blast the third mile,” said Stafford-Kirk. “He moved from 20th to 18th to 13th through the race. I am so proud of Frankie’s dedication to training which got him here today! Making the NE meet is tough- and he is one of a few Bristol runners to to do it over the past couple decades."

Hall was able to snare the Open title behind an 86-point showing followed by Ridgefield (113), Danbury (120) and New Milford (134).

The 2022 NE Championship takes place next Saturday at Ponagansett High School in North Scituate, Rhode Island and Porrini will be part of an exclusive field.
