Stunning views and bright autumn colors await hikers at Indian Rock Nature Preserve

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=18954 Published on 2022-10-25 07:00:00

By Laura Bailey

Autumn leaves crunch underfoot throughout the shady hiking trails at Indian Rock Nature Preserve. The large oak trees provide robust canopy boasting striking shades of yellow, orange and scarlet.

A bright red covered bridge greets hikers on the beginning of their journey. A small observation deck is located just past the bridge, allowing hikers an intimate view of the surrounding area.

The hiking trails encircle a large lake with several docks that are well-used by swimmers in the summertime. As you travel along the trails, youâ€™ll see dragonflies skimming the water, frogs hopping by the shore and squirrels gathering food for the long winter ahead.

In the woods are two authentic Wigwams, the pebble house and a quiet area to sit by the lake.

The trails at Indian Rock Nature Preserve are not open to the public.

Temperatures in the mid-60s this week will provide warm conditions for leaf peeping during this peak week.
