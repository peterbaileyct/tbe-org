St. Paul goes to 4-0 in a 20-0 mercy-shortened game at Kennedy of Waterbury

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=24805 Published on 2023-04-11 15:07:38

Kyle Wilson picked up his second win in as many starts as the St. Paul Falcons defeated the Kennedy Eagles 20-0 in Waterbury Monday afternoon. Wilson pitched the first 4 innings scattering 3 hits while striking out 7.

Mitch Crowley, making his varsity debut, pitched the final inning. Crowley retired all 3 Kennedy batters to face him striking out 2.

Starter Brian Harrington took the loss for Kennedy.

St Paul scored its first run in the top of the first inning. Jamie Leggett singled, stole 2nd, and came in to score on Cal Chance's RBI single.

St Paul took advantage of a dropped fly ball to score six unearned runs in the second inning. The inning was highlighted by an RBI single by Wilson, a two RBI double by Leggett, and an inside the park three run homer by Chance.

Singles by Cole Raymond and Wilson set the table for St Paul in the third inning. Leggett delivered another 2 RBI double and Chance drove in Leggett to make it 10-0.

In the top of the fifth, St Paul took advantage of 4 walks, 2 hit batters, and 2 errors to score 10 runs on only 3 hits: an RBI single by Raymond, a 2 RBI double by Logan Rembish, and the first varsity hit and RBI on a single by freshman Tony DeSena. St Paul led 20-0, and that was the final  score.

Of Note:
For St Paul

Cal Chance was three for four with a three run homer, six RBIs, and two runs scored. Jamie Leggett was three for four with two doubles, four RBIs, and three runs scored. Kyle Wilson was two for three with two RBIs and three runs scored. And Cole Raymond was two for four with one RBI.
