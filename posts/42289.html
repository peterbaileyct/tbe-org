Boys & Girls Club of Bristol names 2024 Youth of the Year

Over the past few months, Josie assembled a personal portfolio to be reviewed by a panel of judges. On the day of the Youth of the Year ceremony, she gave a speech in front of the panel and then participated in a 15-minute interview.

When asked about the impact The Club had on her life Josie replied, “It was the place that I knew I could always fall back on. It was a place where I knew I could go and find someone that always wanted the best for me.”

Josie said she first started coming to the Boys & Girls Club of Bristol when she was enrolled in our School-Based Programs while attending elementary school and has been a member of The Club for 10 years. Now a member of our prestigious Keystone Club, a club within the club for exemplary teen leaders, Josie uses her knowledge and skills to lead and inspire youth to dream big.

Josie, who is currently a sophomore attending Bristol Central High School, is looking forward to her new role as Youth of the Year.

Since 1948, Youth of the Year has been Boys & Girls Clubs of America’s premier recognition program. It is a celebration of the extraordinary achievements of club teens and is the highest honor a "Club Kid" can receive.

Club members who earn the Youth of the Year title embody the values of leadership service, academic excellence, and healthy lifestyles. They exemplify the critical impact that Boys Girls Clubs have on the lives of young people.

Being named the 2024 Bristol Youth of the Year means that Josephine will serve as both an exemplary ambassador for Club youth, and as a strong voice for teens in the community.

The Boys & Girls Club of Bristol has provided services to children and families in the Greater Bristol area for over 100 years. Our long-standing services are enhanced by the innovative and highly effective programs provided by Imagine Nation, A Museum Early Learning Center. Serving over 50,000 children and their families each year, the organization strives to inspire and enable all young people, especially those who need us the most, to realize their full potential as productive, responsible, and caring citizens.
