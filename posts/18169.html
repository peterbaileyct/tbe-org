Candidate Hahn offers incumbent Martin an opportunity to meet the public next Wednesday, Oct. 19

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=18169 Published on 2024-11-20 09:47:40

Greg Hahn, the Democratic candidate for the 31st Senate District, has reserved two hours at the Bristol Public Library next week on Wednesday, Oct. 19, from 6 to 8 p.m., to give the voters a chance to meet and ask questions. He has invited his opponent, Republican incumbent Henri Martin to attend also, but as of this time has not received a reply from Martin.

Hahn has said in his promotional material that he is hoping this forum can in some way compensate for the total lack of any scheduled debates this year. The Chamber of Commerce usually hosts a debate between the candidates but will not be doing so in this election cycle.

Martin's anti-abortion views are sure to be one of the issues discussed

Hahn has reminded the public on his Facebook page that Martin was the largest contributor to the organization Celebrate Life, which was an anti-choice organization from the 1980's with the intent of abolishing abortion in Connecticut.

According to news reports at the time, Celebrate Life was proposing to:

  • Force women who had abortions to register with City Hall
  • Track women who had an abortion
  • Mandate anesthesia and burial for fetal tissue
  • Lead to the overturning of Roe v. Wade and end reproductive freedom.

Hahn's press release includes that information.

In just this past legislative session, Public Act 22-19 was passed by the legislature. It is a first-in-the-nation law that will protect medical providers and patients seeking abortion care in Connecticut who may be traveling from other states that have outlawed abortion. Additionally, the law expands abortion access in Connecticut by expanding the type of practitioners eligible to perform certain abortion-related care.

Martin and Republican counterpart in House District 77 Cara Pavalock-D'amato voted no. Whit Betts (R-78) who is retiring his seat in the state house, was absent and did not vote. 

Martin was contacted by TBE and asked to comment. He had not replied at the time of publication.
