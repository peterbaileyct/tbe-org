Eastern's Fitzsimons won the Class L title in the javelin as the BC and BE girls track & field programs competed at the state championships on Wednesday

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=13834 Published on 2022-06-04 13:14:09

By Michael Letendre

NEW BRITAIN - The girls outdoor track and field teams from Bristol Central and Bristol Eastern had tremendous showings at the CIAC Class L event from Willow Brook Park in New Britain on Wednesday.

The Rams ended up in 14th place with 24 points while the Lancers took 17th - scoring 12 points.

Edwin O. Smith won the state title behind 78.5 points while Simsbury - just like their male counterparts - was the runner-up at the event.

Shelton placed third, just six points behind the Trojans (64).

The top five finishers in each event qualified for the State Open event and athletes from both Central and Eastern will continue on in the postseason.

Included in the group is a newly minted state champion from the Lancers’ program.

Eastern’s Mackenzie Fitzsimons won the title in the javelin with a throw of 119 feet, six inches.

At the Open, only Shea Green from Weston (124 feet, five inches) and Alice Stettinger of East Haven (120-05) are seeded higher than Fitzsimons.

For Central, Kylee Barnes took third in the long jump (16 feet, 10 inches) and fourth in the high jump (five feet) - qualifying for the states in each event.

Earning fifth place for the Rams was Beccy Litwinczyk in the 200 meters (27.08 seconds) as did the 4x100 meter rally team of Emily Blanchette, Litwinczyk, Francesca Amara, and Lynae Lewis (51.94 seconds).

Central’s 4x400 meter team of  Sophia Stark, Litwinczyk, Amara, and Lewis finished in seventh place (4:19.26) while for Eastern, the grouping of Cecelia Gilbert, Kaydence Mayer, Lauren Ayotte, and Fiona Gallagher took seventh place in the 4x800 meter relay (10:19.10).

Lewis placed ninth in the 400 meters (101.95) and Destinee Craft placed 20th in the long jump (14-3).

To round out the day for the Rams, Barnes (17th, 30-10.5) and Craft (18th, 29-7) performed well in the triple jump while Sophia Stark finished in 26th place in the 800 meters (2:40.41).

The 2022 CIAC State Open Championship commences on Monday, June 6 from Willow Brook Park in New Britain.
