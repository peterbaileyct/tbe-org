Where to see fall foliage this week in Bristol

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=17375 Published on 2022-09-21 16:00:00

By Laura Bailey

The final rays of summer sun are illuminating early pops of autumn foliage in Bristol.  

Some red maples in higher elevations have started to turn lovely shades of autumn burgundy, and more sugar maples are showing orange hues around their crowns.

There hasn’t been a huge change in Bristol fall foliage over the past week due to warm daytime temperatures and an abundance of sunlight.

The forecast for the upcoming week shows the classic early fall temperature dip. Daytime highs are predicted to reach the low 70s, while overnight lows will drop into the low 40s. We’ll see how next week’s weather impacts Bristol’s color palette. 

Recommended Walk for Leaf Peeping: Queen Street to High Street

This walk is exactly one mile up and down several hills. The neighborhood’s high elevation provides a great view of Memorial Park Boulevard and the changing foliage in the distance.
