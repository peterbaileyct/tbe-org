Five-run fifth carries Bristol Eastern to third straight win over Plainville on Wednesday

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=44577 Published on 2024-04-25 10:06:44

The Bristol Eastern baseball team won their third consecutive game on Wednesday, with a comeback win over Plainville at Clem Roy Field, winning by the score of 5-3.

Eastern trailed 2-0 going into the bottom of the fifth, and then sent 10 batters to the plate, scoring five runs, and taking a 5-2 lead.  With one out, Gabe White singled, stole second, went to third on a wild pitch, and scored when Noah Molinsky reached on an infield error.  Ben D’Amato followed with a single, and both runners scored on Owen Myers double to deep left-center. A walk to Preston Guarda, and RBI singles by Mason Harris and Jonny Montgomery resulted in a 5-2 Lancer lead after five innings of play.

Plainville had taken a 2-0 lead, scoring a run in the third on an RBI infield single by Tate Stevens off Eastern starter Anthony Nocera, and getting their second run in the top of fifth on another Stevens rbi single off reliever Jonny Montgomery.

 Nocera pitched the first four innings for Eastern, while Montgomery threw the final three, picking up his first win of the season.

Nocera allowed three hits and one run, striking out six and also walking six.  Montgomery allowed two runs,  four hits, and struck out two with no walks.  Plainville left ten runners on base.

For Eastern, Ben D’Amato had a perfect day at the plate,  with three singles and an intentional walk.
