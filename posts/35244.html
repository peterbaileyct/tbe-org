Main St. cannabis establishment, grant to clean up former car dealership site on council agenda

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=35244 Published on 2023-10-10 13:59:00

 CITY COUNCIL MEETING – TUESDAY, OCTOBER 10, 2023

7:00 P.M. – CITY HALL COUNCIL CHAMBERS

111 NORTH MAIN STREET, BRISTOL

REVISED AGENDA

1.    Opening Ceremonies.

2.    Approval of minutes of the regular City Council Meeting of September, 2023. CC 2

3.    Public Participation.

4.    Announcements.

5.    Consent Calendar. 

a.    To place on file the New Hire Report for September, 2023. CC 5a

b.    To approve the Tax Refunds dated September 27, 2023. CC 5b

c.    To approve the applications for the FY24 Distracted Driving High Visibility Enforcement Grant, 2024 DUI Enforcement Grant, Click It or Ticket Grant, and the Speed & Aggressive Driving Enforcement Grant. CC 5c

d.    To award Contract 2P24-015 Personal Property Tax Audits to Tax Management Associates, Inc. and to refer to the Office of Corporation Counsel for contract review, and to authorize the Mayor or Acting Mayor to execute any and all documents necessary to effect said contract. CC 5d

e.    To approve the submission by the Parks, Recreation, Youth & Community Services Department for a $7,141.55 Local Prevention Council grant from the CT Department of Mental Health and Addiction Services’ Prevention and Health Promotion Division, in collaboration with the Regional Behavioral Health Action Organizations, to support the local prevention council – BEST – with vaping prevention efforts in Bristol. CC 5e

f.    To authorize the Mayor or Acting Mayor to execute any and all documents associated with the City of Bristol’s Local Emergency Medical Services Plan. CC 5f

g.    To award Contract 2P24-004 Stewardship Permit Implementation for Bristol Landfill to WSP USA Inc. in the amount of $45,740.00 and to authorize the Mayor or Acting Mayor to execute any and all documents necessary to effect said contract. CC 5g

h.    To award Contract 2P24-011 Asset Management Software to Brightly Software, Inc. in the amount of $54,384.70 and to authorize the Mayor or Acting Mayor to execute any and all documents necessary to effect said contract. CC 5h

i.    To award Contract 2024-019 Managed Detection and Response Software to Tyler Technologies, Inc. in the amount of $145,000.00 and to authorize the Mayor or Acting Mayor to execute any and all documents necessary to effect said contract. CC 5i

6.    Reports and Committee Reports.

a.    Salary Committee – To approve a change to the part-time position of Administrative Assistant – Legal (Local 233, Salary Code 6) to full time effective October 11, 2023 and to refer to the Board of Finance for funding. CC 6a

b.    Ordinance Committee - To adopt amendments to the Bristol Code of Ordinances, Chapter 5, Article II Permits and Permit Fees, Sec. 5-21.2 Failure to Secure Building Permit. Fines and Penalties.  CC 6b

7.    Old Business.

8.    New Business.  

9.    Resignations.

10.    Appointments.

11.    To award Contract 2C24-012 Kern Park Improvements in the amount of $215,800.00 and to authorize the Mayor or Acting Mayor to execute any and all documents necessary to effect said contract. CC 11

12.    To approve a resolution establishing a Senior Volunteer Tax Relief Program for senior citizens who volunteer their services to qualifying entities in the City. CC 12

13.    To approve and authorize New England Edibles, LLC to operate as a Cannabis Establishment at 159 Main Street, Bristol pursuant to City of Bristol Ordinance section 13-126. CC 13

14.    To authorize the Mayor or Acting Mayor to provide a letter of endorsement for Mr. Bhrugesh Patel’s application for a $1,100,000 Brownfield remediation grant through the Naugatuck Valley Council of Governments for environmental cleanup activities at 40 West Street, formerly known as Chic Miller Chevrolet. CC 14

15.    To authorize the Mayor or Acting Mayor to execute a contract for electricity generation for the period from July 1, 2024 through June 30, 2027, as recommended by the Purchasing Agent and/or the CT Conference of Municipalities. CC 15

16.    Executive Session to discuss litigation matter of SBK, LLC v. City of Bristol, Superior Court Docket No. HHB-CV-20-6059735-S and to take any action as necessary. CC 16

17.    Executive Session to discuss litigation matter of 570 Main Street, LLC v. City of Bristol, Superior Court Docket No. HHB-CV-20-6059736-S and to take any action as necessary. CC 17

18.    Any other business to come before said meeting.

19.    Adjournment.

Erica Cabiya

Town and City Clerk

All TBE readers, supporters and donors                

The Bristol Edition will be limiting the number of stories non-members and free readers may access each week. This decision is based on our financial projections and, most certainly, to remind people that TBE is serious about providing accurate, timely and thorough reporting for Bristol. To do this we have devised a financial support structure that makes unlimited access extremely affordable, beginning with a $6 monthly donation.                

  • Non-members will be able to access four (4) articles per week.                
  • Free readers and people who have subscribed by email will be able to access four (4) articles per week.                
  • Donors and financial supporters will have unlimited access as long as they log in.                

Note: Donors may have to contact TBE if they find they are being limited, since we will need to set up a membership account for you. Email editor@bristoledition.org for instructions. Sorry for any inconvenience. People with financial difficulties may write editor@bristoledition.org to be considered for free access.
