Quincy Lawson's move into the starting lineup almost helped BC boys basketball topple Platt on Thursday evening

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=40170 Published on 2024-01-15 16:00:00

By Michael Letendre

BRISTOL - After a recent three game losing streak by the Bristol Central boys basketball team, veteran coach Tim Barrette tweaked his lineup a bit. 

Barrette was going to senior Harry Ross at forward/center and the front court player was producing for the program.

But a little more energy was added to the starting unit when sophomore Quincy Lawson was swapped in for Ross.

“He plays hard. It’s just hard to keep him off the floor,” said Barrette of Lawson. “He’s been earning that spot. Nothing against Harry. I need Harry to play a ton still. He’ll still play his minutes.”

The switch is a good one because now Ross can come off the pine to rebound, block shots and put in a couple misses to lead the second unit while Lawson - like the Energizer Bunny - seemingly has a motor that doesn’t quit.

At 2-7 overall this year, Barrette was hoping that the athletic Lawson would move the needle against an equally positioned program such as Platt (2-6).

Lawson averages 6.4 points-per-game, third on the team, but his lay-ups and put-backs are just part of the equation.

He can rebound over anyone, he’ll hit the hardwood without a thought and those hustle points are mentally tracked by Barrette and his staff.

Against Platt last Thursday, Lawson posted seven points, grabbing two early offensive rebounds and the baskets off those misses gave Central a 23-12 edge over Platt with 5:38 to go in the first half.

His hustle and guile made a big difference over the first half of play before Central eventually fell to Platt 59-57.

“I just thought with the way Platt goes after the ball, I needed a different spark,” said Barrette. “With Quincy’s energy, I thought that was the best move for tonight to try to get off to a good start and honestly, we did.”

“That move definitely paid off tonight.”

Over the fall, everyone saw what Lawson brought to Central’s football program for head coach Jeff Papazian.

That ability helped him achieve on the football field and in hoop from the Charles C. Marsh Gymnasium on the campus of BCHS.

He can score as his team-leading 11 points against Bristol Eastern during the BC Holiday Invitational nearly got the job accomplished against the Lancers.

There’s not a ball out of his reach or an open player he can’t get to defensively when the sophomore is in the area.

Frankly, Lawson might just be scratching the surface in Barrette’s offensive and defensive schemes.

“The one thing I know is that he’s going to literally leave everything he has on the floor, good or bad,” said Barrette.
