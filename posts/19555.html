If you have not registered, you still can register and vote on Tuesday

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=19555 Published on 2022-11-07 07:00:00

Election Day Registration

The pre-election voter registration cutoff deadline is seven (7) days before Election Day. So, you must have registered as of last Tuesday to be eligible to vote at your polling place.

But Connecticut also has same day registration on Election Day itself for voters who need it.

Election Day Registration permits anyone to register and vote in person on Election Day:

  • who meets the eligibility requirements for voting in this state and is not already registered.
    By law, a person is eligible to register and vote if he or she is (1) a U.S. citizen, (2) age 18 or older, (3) a bona fide resident of the town in which he or she applies for admission, and (4) has completed confinement if previously convicted of a disfranchising felony.
  • or is registered in one town but has moved to another town.

Election Day Registration is not available at your polling place but is available at a designated EDR location in each town, beginning at 6 a.m. and ending at 8 p.m. The EDR location in Bristol this year is City Hall WEST, 131 North Main Street, 2nd Floor.

Please plan to arrive early in the day as there may be long lines. If you are in line at the Election Day Registration site at 8 p.m., you can remain in line to complete your registration and vote. You will need to provide proof of identity and residency.
