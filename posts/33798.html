Bristol Central and Bristol Eastern cross country teams hit the course for the first time on Saturday at the Stratton Brook Part Invite

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=33798 Published on 2023-09-11 07:00:00

By Michael Letendre

SIMSBURY - The Bristol public high schools were in the mix at the Stratton Brook Cross Country Invitational from Simsbury on Saturday.

And at Stratton Brook Park, the 3.1-mile course saw Bristol Central’s girls program top Bristol Eastern while on the boys side, the Lancers were a few places better than the Rams.

It’s been a tough start to the season with the heat and practice being what it was due to the treacherous conditions.

On the girls side in Simsbury, the Rams finished fifth overall in the large school division while the Lancers placed seventh.

Central’s wheelhouse of Sophia Stark (11th, 24:07) and Katie Tran (14th, 24:44) weren’t far off the top-10 pack while teammate Emma Cavallari (22nd, 26:09) also ran well.

For Eastern, senior Lauren Ayotte (31st, 27:38) was the top finisher for the Kingstreeters.

She was followed in order by Calliope Gallagher (32nd, 27:47) and Rita Gao (33rd, 28:07).

Windsor, with 47 points, won the girls event while Norwich Free Academy (41) earned the nod for the boys.

For the boys, Eastern finished sixth, two spots ahead of the Rams (eighth).

Individually, Patrick Wininger from Central was a top-five finisher.

He placed fourth overall with a stellar time of 17:55 while teammate Rowan Hennessey (35th, 20:38) and Braydan Dwyer (38th, 20:58) showed some gumption on the course.

“[It was a] good day,” said Central coach Tamara Stafford-Kirk. “We have a long way to go but I’m encouraged by our performances, especially with a smaller team this year. Patrick Wininger had a great run finishing fourth and for the girls Sophia Stark and Katie Tran got us off to a strong start. We also had some super JV performances led by Beckham Barse.”

“Our freshmen look strong too led by Noah Pellerin, Naythan Pellerin, and Emersyn Mazzei.”

The future is once again bright for the Central cross country programs.

The top three runners for the Lancers boys included Jase Koczur (20th, 19:32), Cole Beland (24th, 20:00) and Brendan Tartarelli (33rd, 20:34).

This week, Central is at Hubbard Park in Meriden to battle Plainville and host Platt in a CCC South showdown at 3:45 p.m.
