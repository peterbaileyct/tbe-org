'Pass the Purse' donations exceed $6,000, fund senior women's personal care needs

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=48236 Published on 2024-07-11 12:30:00

This year’s Main Street Community Foundation's "Pass the Purse" fundraiser with its $6,053 in donations resulted in six grants to local organizations to assist with the purchase of personal care items for senior women.

Receiving grants were the following:

  • · Bristol Senior Center - $1,553
  • · Burlington Social Services - $600
  • · Plainville Senior Center - $900
  • · Plymouth Community Food Pantry - $600
  • · Southington Community Services - $1,500
  • · Town of Wolcott - $900

The fundraiser took place at the Foundation's annual Women of Wonder this past May.

At the event, which celebrates the Women & Girls Fund and the grants made throughout the years, small purses were placed on each table and passed from one attendee to the next for donations to support a specific cause.

This year’s cause, inspired by the needs identified by local organizations that serve women and girls, was personal care items for senior women in the towns and cities the foundation serves -- Bristol, Burlington, Plainville, Plymouth, Southington and Wolcott.

Roughly one in three older adults aged 65+ are economically insecure, and senior women are more likely to live in poverty than men, according to the release announcing the grants.

Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) dollars cannot be used for personal care items and therefore many women simply go without because these products can be expensive. The top requests by senior women in the local communities are disposable washcloths, denture adhesive, incontinence products, shampoo and body wash and deodorant.

Main Street Community Foundation has awarded a total of $780,000 in grants from the Women & Girls Fund to organizations and individuals in Bristol, Burlington, Plainville, Plymouth, Southington and Wolcott since the Fund’s establishment in 2001.

For more information on the Women & Girls Fund, contact the Main Street Community Foundation at 860.583.6363 or visit mainstreetfoundation.org/women-girls-fund.
