Caminiti weekend -- pending

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=52373 Published on 2024-10-15 20:19:49

By Neal Supranovich, American Legion Post 2 historian

 The weekend of Oct 12th and 13th was a special weekend in Bristol. Two Birthday parties were held to honor Joe Caminiti, World War II Veteran, last known Iwo Jima Survivor in Ct. and who witnessed the raising of the U.S. Flag on Iwo Jima during the Battle for the Island. Joe turned 100.

    Saturday Oct. 12th members of the New Britain Marine Corp League hosted a Birthday party at American Legion, Post 2 where Joe is a member. The event was open to the public. Members of the Marine Corp League from across Ct. attended.

   The event started with honoring John Fensick, being 98 wedding. At this wedding, John and Dick Fitz, being 99 and Joe Caminiti, 100 became long time friends thanks to their girlfriends planning.

    The Marine Corp League read and presented a Certificate from Senator Blumenthal's Office along with presenting a Iwo Jima Coin that has been handed down through the members of the New Britain Marine Corp League.   A bagpiper played the Marine Corp Hymn in Joe's Honor.

     Next our State Rep. Cara Pavalock D'Amato and Mary Fortier with support from State Rep. Joe Hoxha and State Senator Henri Martin presented a proclamation on behalf of the State of Ct. to Joe.

   A proclamation was read by Vice Commander, Department of Ct, American Legion, Mike Kellett. The proclamation was from the National Commander. Members from American Legion Post 2, Post 20 and Post 209 were on hand for the reading. The Vice Commander also read a personnel Birthday card from the National Commander to Joe.

     Members of the World War II Legacy Foundation with the help of the Chairman of the Ct. The Iwo Jima Survivors Association presented Joe with a proclamation. The Founder of "Veterans Are Heroes Forever", Dan DiVirgilio made Joe an honorary member. After singing a round of Happy Birthday started by the mayor, Joe cut his cake Marine Corp style with a Saber.

     Sunday Oct. 13th a Dinner Party was held at Max's Dining Hall early that afternoon. Joe was met by some 80 family and close friends. At this event the Mayor of Bristol read a proclamation declaring Oct. 13th Joe Caminiti Day in Bristol.  The Mayor also presented a Key to the City to Joe. Who had no idea what to do with it?  The Mayor and John Lodovico quilted Joe with a quilt he received a few years ago. This allowed those present to see it for the first time.

        The World War II Legacy Foundation presented Joe with a homemade card signed by all those there for both events, Saturday and Sunday. The Legacy Foundation arranged for Joe to become a member of the Third Marine Division Association.  Joe was presented a membership card along with a special frame picture of the raising of the Flag. His story will appear in an upcoming issue of the Third Division's magazine.

        The Legacy Foundation next quilted Joe's wife of 78 years, Gerry Caminiti.

        Many there said words to affect Joe were a great person.

      John, a long time friend and Vietnam veteran, had the most important thing to say.  During the Battle of Iwo Jima Joe transported the wounded. Joe took it upon himself, at 18. If the Marine was severely wounded, not take him to the rear Aid Station but directly to the Hospital Ship without orders. If they did not have room to take the wounded Marine then Joe took him to another Medical Ship. A number of Marines' lives were saved thanks to Joe's actions during the battle.  John wondered if he could have been that cool at 18.  

      Monday morning while working off what he ate Sunday; Channel 8 crew interviewed Joe while working out at the GYM.
