Registrars of Voters announces deadlines for unaffiliated voter changes, new registrations prior to Aug. 13 state Republican primary

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=47665 Published on 2024-06-26 07:00:00

The office of the Registrars of Voters is available for residents who would like to register to vote for the first time or any unaffiliated voters wishing to change their party affiliation in order to participate in the Aug. 13 state Republican primary.

The office encourages voters to make any necessary changes to current or new voter registration status online by using the web address below: https://voterregistration.ct.gov/OLVR/welcome.do.

New voters may submit mail-in applications and must be postmarked or received by Registrar of Voters by July 26. For unaffiliated voters, the mail-in application must be received (not merely postmarked) by the Registrar of Voters by July 26. In-person registration of a new voter or an unaffiliated voter will be accepted until 12 noon on Aug. 12.

Please note that the party transfer deadline was May 15, and anyone transferring from one party to another after this date is not eligible to vote in the state Republican primary.

Residents can also check their status online at www.dir.ct.gov/sots/lookup.aspx or call the office at (860) 584-6165.

The Registrars of Voters Office hours are Monday through Thursday, 8 a.m. to 5 p.m.; Friday, 8:30 a.m. to 12 p.m. All polling locations will be open on Primary Day, Tuesday, Aug. 13, from 6 a.m. to 8 p.m.
