Lusitani out as city councilor: Resigns at city council meeting

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=24075 Published on 2023-03-16 07:00:00

By Laura Bailey

Bristol City Councilor Jolene Lusitani (R, District 1) announced her resignation Tuesday evening during the monthly city council meeting. 

“As of March 31st, I will be resigning from City Council,” said Lusitani. “I am very honored to have this amazing opportunity with my fellow councilmen and mayor. It was such a wonderful, humble and educational experience, and I have met so many great people that live and the town of Bristol.

“I have formed wonderful relationships, and I'm looking forward to continuing them,” Lusitani added. “Bristol is a big small town that has a community that rises up to every occasion, and I couldn't be prouder. So, a big thank you to everyone, and I know whoever fills this seat will have the same wonderful experience as I did.”

Lusitani, who took office in November 2021, is one of six first-term Republican city councilors. 

Mayor Jeffrey Caggiano and fellow District 1 City Councilor Sebastian Panioto complimented and thanked Lusitani for her service. 

“All the effort that you put in, it’s not easy serving as you have,” said Caggiano. “Thank you very much; we will miss you.”

“It's been a pleasure to serve with you in the first district,” said Panioto. “You brought a lot of energy, and you were well organized in what you did. It’s been great serving with you, and I wish you all the best in the future.”
