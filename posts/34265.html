Saturday night, the 620-seat Rockwell Theatre debuted, and it was a success by all accounts, even with the power outage. This will be a prelude of things, with more memorable evenings in the months and years to come.

The theatre has been described as a jewel and the gateway to downtown. You will get no disagreement here, but it was almost not to be, however.

The land was donated by Albert Rockwell to the city in 1920, and the school followed in 1921. By 2012, it had worn out its usefulness and was closed.

A robust debate then ensued regarding its future. Thankfully, the art magnet school (BAIMS) and theatre won out.

It would have been devastating if the building had become city hall or a stale high-end condominium complex where only an elite few benefits from the building and its 7.4 acres.
