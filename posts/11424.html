Eastern and Central wrestling duked it out against the state's best at the Class L championships this past weekend

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=11424 Published on 2022-02-21 09:19:57

By Michael Letendre

WILTON -The Class L field in scholastic wrestling is always a loaded one and over Friday and Saturday, both the programs from Bristol Central and Bristol Eastern did well at the annual championship event.

Wrestling was shelved last year due to the pandemic and the locals were eager in hitting the mats at the Class L championships from Wilton high school.

Xavier of Middletown won the title, scoring 318.5 points while runner-up Simsbury chimed in with 234.5.

Both squads are usual state powers in wrestling and the program from Middletown ramped up five first place wins and four second place finishes.

The Trojans had two champs, a single victor for second and third and captured four fourth place finishes.

Eastern finished in ninth place overall with 91 points while Central took 11th, collecting 72 points.

The Lancers tallied one second and two fifth place finishes while the Rams earned a second and a sixth over a busy day of action.

A couple local grapplers were in the finals as Central’s Will Hamilton (120 pounds) and Eastern’s Alex Marshall (160) just missed out on individual state titles.

Hamilton battled Xavier’s Jackson Heslin through three periods, dropping a tough 8-5 decision and then at 160, Edward Barlage (Guilford) just upended Marshall via a 3-2 decision.

The Lancers earned two fifth place titles as Gabe White (126) earned a pin-fall win over Jack Wiesbrock from Daniel Hand in 2:20 while Dylan Piazza (182) pinned Joseph Della Ripa of South Windsor in 1:44.

And then for the Rams, Umar Malick (152) fell to Daniel Hand’s Cameron Chieppo in the fifth place bout - pinned at 3:55 of the second period.
