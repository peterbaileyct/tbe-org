Early voting for Republican primary to continue through Sunday

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=49298 Published on 2024-08-08 07:00:00

Early voting for the Republican primary for the candidate who will face Democrat Chris Murphy in November for a U.S. Senate seat began Monday and will continue through Sunday.

Hours for early voting are Thursday, 8 a.m. to 8 p.m. and Friday through Sunday, Aug. 11, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Early voting is held in city council chambers at city hall.

The endorsed candidate is Gerry Smith who is facing challenger Matt Corey. To qualify, Corey received 15 percent of the votes at the Republican convention.

The primary will take place on Tuesday, Aug. 13. All polling locations will be open 6 a.m. to 8 p.m. The primary is open only to registered Republicans.

"One thing to note is Northeast Middle School is under construction, so the Stevens Street entrance will be closed," said assistant registrar Jennifer Brunoli. "Voters must use the Felice Road entrance."

In addition, Brunoli said there is an assisted voting machine at all locations for hearing/sight impaired individuals. Every privacy both has wheelchair accessibility as does each polling station entrance, per state statue.
