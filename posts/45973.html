Lewis Mills knocks Bristol Eastern out of the CCC Lacrosse Tournament Monday

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=45973 Published on 2024-05-22 07:00:00

The Bristol Eastern girls lacrosse team hosted the Spartans from Lewis Mills Monday night in a CCC semi-final, and the Lady Lancers were unable to defend their home turf as a 14-8 loss allowed the Spartans to move on. 

A second-half Mills surge that BE had no answer brought on Eastern’s demise.

The Lancers only trailed by one at the half, 5-4, but that one goal deficit ballooned to 10-5 at the end of the third quarter. 

The fourth period saw both teams trade goals which led to the final score of 14-8.

Erin Martin made several key saves throughout to keep the Lancers in the game without two starting defenders in front of her.  Jayna Hatcher and Meg Krok were left to battle with a variety of substitute players at their sides and the Lewis Mills offense exposed those vulnerabilities.

The Lancers were paced by Harper Dauphinee and Emma Stavens with three goals apiece.  Vanessa Drury and Kate Patterson each pitched in with a goal. 

The Lancers picked up a last-second goal at the end of the third quarter as Savannah Drury connected from the goal line on a pass to Emma Stavens.  That goal seemed to spur the Lancer's momentum as they picked up two more to make it 10-7, but the Spartans quickly answered with three of their own to make it 13-7. 

In the final few minutes, both teams played end-to-end and traded goals for the final score of 14-8.

Bristol Eastern now awaits their seeding for the state tournament which will begin next week.
