REQUEST FOR PROPOSALS FOR THE REDEVELOPMENT OF THE J.H. SESSIONS AND SON BUILDING

The City is accepting submissions from developers and associated parties for the transfer of ownership and redevelopment of an approximately 3.54-acre property commonly known as the J.H. Sessions and Son Building at 273 Riverside Avenue, Bristol CT (the “Property”). The City intends to transition this environmentally remediated property from an abandoned mill building to a vibrant workforce housing apartment community. The Request for Proposals and other related materials are available on the City’s website: http://www.bristolct.gov/bids.
 
Proposals will be accepted by the City of Bristol Purchasing Department until 1:00 pm, April 16, 2024. Submissions received after that time will not be considered.

Abou the Property
 Bristol is leading a complete revitalization of the Riverside Avenue corridor, an effort known locally as the “Riverside Revival.” Riverside Avenue runs parallel with the popular Memorial Boulevard Park and is a key gateway to the City’s reenergized downtown. The City is embarking on a State funded, complete streetscape reconstruction for Riverside Avenue while encouraging new development and business activity along the corridor. Redevelopment of the 80,000 square-foot J.H. Sessions and Son building – a handsome brick building with high ceilings, wooden floors, exposed beams, and similar desirable features – stands as the centerpiece of the “Riverside Revival.” The City, together with New Colony Development Corp. landbank and the Connecticut Department of Economic and Community Development, are providing quite literally a “clean slate” for developers to consider: the Property has been remediated to State requirements and is poised for redevelopment as soon as possible.   

A video containing additional information about this property can be viewed here: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VJU7A-8CeCg

Submissions may be withdrawn 120 days after opening if no award has been made. The City reserves the right to waive any informalities in any proposal, to reject any or all proposals, and to accept the proposal that in its judgment is in its best interest. The selected proposer will take affirmative action to ensure that applicants are employed, and that employees are treated during employment without regard to their race, color, religion, gender, or national origin.
