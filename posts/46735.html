Brand new Seymour Park playground celebrated with ribbon cutting

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=46735 Published on 2024-06-06 13:59:00

By Maria Caceres

Superintendent, Dr. Joshua Medeiros, of the Bristol Parks, Recreation, Youth and Community Services stood among kids, alongside a representative of the company responsible for designing the new playground at Seymour Park, several members of both the city council and Board of Park Commissioners and the mayor for the ribbon cutting of the new playground yesterday.

The project is part of a master plan to improve the neighborhood parks, Medeiros said. He said it was supported by a board of park commissioners, city council and board of finance members.

The design of the playground came from Kompan, a playground design company, represented at the ribbon cutting by Summer Berube, the territory manager.

“I just know it’s [the park] gonna be loved for many years,” Berube said.

Two of the major offerings of the new playground at Seymour Park include a castle and a ship for children aged 2 to 5 years. | Maria Caceres

The Mayor Jeffrey Caggiano said Bristol should be called "Park City."

“We [the city] should be called the Park City here in Bristol,” he said. “With all the improvements over the years, we certainly rival other cities that use that [title].”

Caggiano said that he was proud to call the neighborhood his and to have raised his children there.

He said he had received some positive comments about the updated park already.
