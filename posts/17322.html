The Top-10 poll in scholastic football for Week 3 sees several squads that will be in CCC action this weekend

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=17322 Published on 2022-09-20 16:00:00

By Michael Letendre

The most recent Connecticut scholastic Top-10 in football was just released for Week 3 and while no local squad made the list, several heavy hitters populate the 10 best teams in the state.

All but one (No. 9 Fairfield Prep is 1-1) are a clean 2-0 while multiple programs received at least one first place vote in this week’s poll.

Greenwich is in first place with 670 points, earning the top spot once again.

The squad picked up 15 first place votes but must contend against No. 6 Southington (431 points) in a huge non-conference bout in Greenwich this weekend.

No. 2 New Canaan (604 points, one first place vote) is also undefeated and battles No. 9 Fairfield Prep (362 votes) in another Top-10 confrontation on Friday night.

Shelton (576 points, five first place votes) comes in at No. 3 and takes its shot at Windsor (1-1) on Friday night. The Warriors are one of the top twenty programs in the state.

No. 4 Killingly (472 votes, one first place vote) stays home to tackle Bloomfield (2-0) in another showcase on Friday while the final program to earn a first place vote, Maloney (444 points), has a big one at Darien (1-1).

After Southington, St. Joseph chimes in at No. 7 (411 points), moving up two slots in this week’s poll. Masuk travels to battle St. Joseph on Saturday at 1:30 p.m.

Trumbull (No. 8, 383 votes) takes on East Hartford on the road on Friday and in the final spot, is there any equal for No. 10 Ansonia in the Naugatuck Valley League?

Ansonia travels to Torrington this Friday at 7 p.m.  

As always in the CT state poll, points are scored on a 30-28-26-24-22-20-18-16-14-12-11-10-9-8-7 basis.

Here are the other squads that earned votes this past week:

West Haven (2-0) 230; Bloomfield (2-0) 208; Darien (1-1) 188; Naugatuck (2-0) 142; Cheshire (2-0) 100; Cromwell/Portland (2-0) 77; Berlin (2-0) 53; Hand (1-1) 43; Windsor (1-1) 42; Masuk (1-1) 41; Wethersfield (2-0) 40; Ridgefield (0-2) 29; Law (2-0) 15; Hamden (2-0) 14; Holy Cross (2-0) 14; New Fairfield (2-0) 8; Windham (2-0) 8.

This week for Bristol Central and Bristol Eastern

This is crossover week for the Central Connecticut Conference and the Rams and Lancers are in the mix (please cash in your bottles and cans for the gas money you’ll need this Friday…).

Central travels to Wilton at 7 p.m. from Fujitani Field while Eastern is in action at Fairfield Warde, also at 7 p.m., from Tetreau/Davis Field.

Wilton enters the non-conference challenge at 2-0 – averaging 27.5 points-per-game while Fairfield Warde is also undefeated (2-0), allowing opponents just 7.5 ppg.

The Rams enter the week at 1-1 while the Lancers (0-2) are looking for their first victory in 2022.
