On this date

It is hard to believe that 50 years ago today (Aug. 1, 1973), Boston Red Sox second baseman, 26-year-old Doug Griffin, hit a lazy fly ball to Felipe Alou in right field to end the bottom of the fifth inning against the New York Yankees. Sigh.

But for Alou and home plate umpire Joe Brinkman, everyone associated with the play is dead. Doug Griffin (2016), pitcher Mel Stottlemyre (2019), catcher Thurman Munson (1979), first base umpire Hank Morgenweck (2007) and even first base coach Don Lenhardt (2014). Sad.

As for the status of the 30,689 in attendance, we are still checking.
