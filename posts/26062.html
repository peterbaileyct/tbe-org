Bristol Eastern baseball foiled by two-out rally as E.O. Smith derails Lancers 5-3 on Monday

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=26062 Published on 2024-11-20 09:47:41

By Michael Letendre 

BRISTOL – Whomever put the hex on the Bristol Eastern baseball squad needs to break it immediately. 

The Lancers, comfortably ahead of CCC interdivisional opponent Edwin O. Smith by a 3-0 push with two outs in the seventh on Monday afternoon, never got to see the fruits of their labor. 

That's because the Panthers scored five runs, all earned, with two outs in the seventh to eventually secure a 5-3 win from Clem J. Roy Field on the campus of Bristol Eastern. 

Smith is now 7-7 overall while Eastern fell to 3-9. 

The teams tied the season series 1-1 and it was a tough luck no decision for Eastern pitcher Caleb Molinsky. 

The chucker threw shutout ball into the seventh, allowing just five hits, striking out seven and walking only one over a great effort. 

He was forced to leave the game in the seventh stanza when he reached the pitch limit while Ryan Maglio was tagged for the loss in relief. 

In that fateful seventh stanza, with the Kingstreeters ahead by three runs, Smith's Joe Willour started the tomfoolery when he lined Molinsky's first pitch into left for a single.   

The next batter flew out to right before Owen Sgro nabbed a base hit to left – putting runners on first and second.   

With Molinsky out of pitches, Maglio came in to throw and his first pitch was popped up to short for the second out of the inning.  

From there, Eastern just couldn’t get that final out before Smith unloaded its offense. 

Maglio walked Trevor Woodmansee to load the bases and when the next batter reached on an infield error, a runner scored to cut the deficit to 3-1. 

When the next batter reached on an infield miscue, another runner tagged the plate, and the game was a one-run affair at 3-2.   

Joe Winakor then singled to right, driving in the tying and go-ahead runs, with a third crossing the plate as the throw in from the outfield went wild.    

To end the half inning, Maglio struck out the final batter, but the Panthers were able to charge out in front by a 5-3 push. 

The Lancers went down in order in the seventh to end the game – propelling Smith to the surprise triumph. 

It wasn’t all bad as Eastern took a 1-0 lead after a scoreless first frame. 

Rio Fernandez was hit by a pitch with one out but a grounder to short by Molinsky forced Fernandez out at second base.   

Kyle Babin then singled to left and when Gabe White reached on an infield single to third, Molinsky scored, and the Lancers earned a 1-0 edge. 

The game was still just a single run affair in Eastern’s favor when the home program added another to the till in the fifth. 

A bunt single by Connor Cyr, coupled with a throwing error on the play, put the runner at second base.   

Ben D'Amato later singled to right to score Cyr, propelling Eastern to a 2-0 push. 

And then the Kingstreeters nabbed yet another run, this time in the sixth to make it a three-run cushion. 

Fernandez opened the sixth with a double over the left fielder's head to get into scoring position and when Molinsky bunted, no one was at first base to record an out, and suddenly, the sacrifice/base hit put runners on the corners.   

Babin then slapped a grounder to third and when the defender attempted to get Fernandez, who was retreating back to bag, he ended up safe as the bases were loaded.   

In a wild sequence, White flew out to center and the throw home to get Fernandez trying to score was on the money for the second out. 

Molinsky moved to third on the play, Babin eventually stole second, and when the pitcher threw wild to second, Molinsky scored for the 3-0 lead that eventually evaporated late in the game.
