Chamber to hold small business resource and educational forum later this month

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=25808 Published on 2023-05-05 07:00:00

The Central Connecticut Chambers of Commerce is sponsoring a Small Business Resource & Educational Forum in Farmington in late May.

The forum includes breakfast followed by a panel discussion and a question-and-answer breakouts from the experts in the small business field.

Panelists include representatives from Connecticut’s Small Business Development Center (SBDC), SCORE of Northwest Connecticut, the U.S. Small Business Administration’ Connecticut office, and HEDCO – CT’s Small Business Edge.

The event will be held at Farmington Gardens, 999 Farmington Ave., Farmington, on Tuesday, May 23, from 8 a.m. to 9:30 a.m. The price is $40 for Chamber members and $50 for non-members.

The Connecticut SBDC is an accredited member of America’s Small Business Development Center network — the most comprehensive small business assistance program in the United States with a proven track record of providing over 40 years of service to small businesses.

SCORE provides a system of mentors who are highly successful and seasoned business professionals and entrepreneurs with the experience and knowledge to help you achieve business goals.

Connecticut’s SBA provides services including funding programs, counseling, federal contracting certifications, and disaster recovery. It also helps connect small business owners to partner organizations, lenders, and other community groups that help small businesses succeed.

HEDCO is a certified Community Development Financial Institution, a U.S. Small Business Administration Microloan lender, as well as an administrator of the Hartford Foundation for Public Giving’s Nonprofit Support Program.

Registration is open. For information, visit the “Chamber Events” section of chamber's website by clicking here: www.centralctchambers.org.
