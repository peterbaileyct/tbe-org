St. Paul loses one run game to Woodland Monday afternoon, drops to 13-3 on the season

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=26026 Published on 2023-05-09 18:28:47

Michael Belcher came within one out of a complete game, and led the Woodland Hawks to a 3-2 win over the St. Paul Falcons in Beacon Falls Monday afternoon.

Belcher allowed only two runs on four hits while striking out eight. Teammate Darren Gasparri faced 1 batter and picked up the one out save.

Starter Logan Rembish took the loss for St. Paul, allowing three runs (two earned) on three hits while striking out three.

Woodland scored the first runs of the game in the bottom of the third inning. With one out, back-to-back walks to Darren Gasparri and Tyler DeCrescenzo put two men on, and a successful double steal put them both in scoring position. A single by Ethan Stepputtis should have scored one, but the ball was bobbled in the outfield and both Gasparri and DeCrescenzo scored.

Three of St. Paul's hits and all of its runs came in the top of the 5th inning. Jonathan Remillard singled to lead off the inning. After one out, Keaton Kroll singled to put two men on. Then with two outs, Jamie Leggett drove a double to the left field fence scoring two and cutting Woodland's lead down to 3-2.
But that was the last hit St Paul would have on the day, and the final score was 3-2.
