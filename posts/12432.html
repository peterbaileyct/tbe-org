Bristol Central girls track draws rare tie, earning 73-73 stalemate against Platt

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=12432 Published on 2022-04-13 12:09:39

By Michael Letendre

BRISTOL - It’s not unheard of but a stalemate in scholastic track and field can occur from time to time.

And it happened to the girls squad from Bristol Central on Tuesday as the Rams tied Platt over a 73-73 final.

The meet in Bristol saw several local athletes earn first place tallies.

The 4x800 squad, consisting of Katie Tran, Maura Waters, Johannie Juarez, and Janelle Brand took first place with a time of 12:32.

And in the 4x400 relay event, Central’s squad roped up the win in 4:38.

Lynae Lewis did a very good job in the 400 meters, finishing first with a time of 1:05.7 and in the two-mile challenge, Waters was back at it again.

In the challenging 3200 meters, Waters was the victor in 15:20.

And then in the field, the Rams dominated the jumping events.

In the long jump, Central’s Destinee Craft scooped up first place with a leap of 15 feet, eight inches.

Then in the high jump, Kylee Barnes made a successful attempt of four feet, six inches.

Barnes made it 2-for-2 when she won the triple jump, snaring the victory with a make of 32 feet and half an inch.

Next Week…

Central travels to Middletown on Tuesday to square off against the Blue Dragons. Meet time is 3:45 p.m.
