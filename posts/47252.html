Bristol American Legion baseball team splits weekend games with a win at Milford and their first loss of the season at Norwalk

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=47252 Published on 2024-06-17 07:00:00

The Bristol American Legion baseball team won their fourth straight game to open the season on Saturday with an 8-3 victory over Milford.

Sunday saw the Bristol bats go quiet for the first time as Norwalk’s Charles Depalma spun a one-hitter in a complete game 7-0 victory for home-standing Norwalk. 6/15/24 Bristol 24 Senior at Milford 24 

Saturday, June 15. Bristol 8- Milford 3

Saturday morning in Milford, Bristol pounded out nine hits and added nine walks in another strong offensive showing, an 8-3 win. Rocco Spirito had a huge double for three RBI’s and Brian Grabowski and Brayden Schenck had two hits apiece to pace the attack.

Caleb Molinsky went 4 and 1/3 innings to get the victory. Molinsky struck out five while giving up six hits

With the win the team’s record improved to 4-0.

Sunday, June 16. Norwalk 7- Bristol 0

Norwalk’s Charles Depalma pitched a one-hit masterpiece against Bristol on Sunday. He struck out eight Bristol hitters, but did allow four bases on balls and two batters were hit by a pitch.

Connor Cyr got the only hit for the Bristol squad and that was a single.

Tom Pirog gave up eight hits and six runs to take the loss, but only two of the six runs for Norwalk were earned.

With the loss, Bristol drops to 4-1.
