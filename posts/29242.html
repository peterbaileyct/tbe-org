Community conversation focuses on mental health

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=29242 Published on 2023-08-02 07:00:00

By Laura Bailey

The Annual Community Conversation will return to Rockwell Park on Thursday for the fourth consecutive year. 

Hosted by the Bristol Parks, Recreation, Youth, and Community Services (BPRYCS), this insightful event aims to raise awareness about mental health and foster an open dialogue within the community.

“We are very pleased to once again be hosting the Award Winning Community Conversation on Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at Rockwell Park,” stated BPRYCS Superintendent Dr. Joshua Medeiros.

“This year the event is focused on mental health, and we are creating a space for the community to come together to not only hear Wes Woodson’s inspiring story, but also to have the opportunity to learn more about the resources in Bristol to support positive mental health.”

The Fourth Annual Community Conversation on diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) will focus on mental health, and will be facilitated by Wes Woodson, a nationally renowned professional public speaker, mental health advocate, and published author.

Over the last three years, Wes has given a TED Talk, published his first book, “I Have Anxiety (So What?)”, and has spoken to thousands of students around the country on the topics of mental health, self-empowerment, and self-love.

Right: The crowd at the Third Annual Community Conversation. Left: Dr. Michael Dietter and Leah McCabe. | Laura Bailey photos

The event will be held Aug. 3, from 5:30-7:30 p.m., at the Rockwell Park Amphitheater. Attendees will have the opportunity to participate in engagement activities that encourage community members to listen, learn and share experiences.

The first 100 participants will receive a free food voucher, as well as Community Conversation t-shirt while supplies last. Admission is free to the public, and attendees are encouraged to bring their own chairs.

The previous year’s Annual Community Conversation was facilitated by Kamora Le’Ella Herrington, who encouraged the crowd to ask one another what a celebration of diversity looks like in their households. Over 100 community members discussed issues of inclusion, diversity and equity while sharing personal experiences throughout the evening.

The BPRYCS’ Community Conversation event received the Inclusive Program of Merit Award from the Connecticut Recreation and Parks Association in 2021, which honors outstanding and ongoing inclusive or adaptive programs that demonstrate a conscious effort to service people of all needs and abilities.

For more information, please visit www.BristolRec.com or call 860-584-6160.
